**"I want to be a data journalist."**

It's been over a year since these words went through my head. At the time, I was an economic research analyst in D.C. with literally zero news experience. A lot has happened since then, and I'm now three months into a ten-month [Knight-Mozilla Fellowship](https://opennews.org/what/fellowships/info/) at the [L.A. Times Data Desk](http://www.latimes.com/local/datadesk/).

I've always believed that you can't know what a job is *really* like until you're  doing it yourself. In my short time as a Fellow, I've already learned so much. 

Some of what I've learned probably falls under "Stuff from Journalism School"; e.g., what "slot" and "slug" mean, how to interview people, who [Strunk and White](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Elements_of_Style) are; or "Stuff from the world of Computer Science / Software Development / Coding"; e.g., [what Django is & why it's so powerful](https://twitter.com/palewire/status/722148515548434432), how [Jupyter notebooks](http://jupyter.org/) can be the future of code-sharing, the pros and cons of Python and R, etc.

Some of it deals with broader questions:


* [What makes something "journalistic"?](https://twitter.com/INNnerds/status/729731688473841665) 
* What makes something "newsworthy" (and is this the same thing as "journalistic")?
* What is "data" journalism, and (why) does it have to be separated from "traditional" journalism? 
* What about ["empirical" journalism](https://twitter.com/natesilver538/status/728621229503057920)?
* Is (data) journalism becoming more like social science, and vice versa?
* If so, [as journalists, how can we "peer review" our data stories?](http://srccon.org/sessions/#proposal-316702)
* What is journalistic instinct, and do I have it?


I'm writing this blog as a way to chronicle my learning, as I sort through these questions and more. I hope it's as helpful to you as it is to me.

And for the record, I'm thrilled that these days, I get to say the words *out loud*:

**"I'm a *journalist*."**
